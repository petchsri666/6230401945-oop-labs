Khon Kaen University (Thai: มหาวิทยาลัยขอนแก่น) or KKU (มข.) is a public research university in Thailand.
It was the first university established in northeastern Thailand
and remains the oldest and largest university in the region.
The university is a hub of education in northeast Thailand.
It is widely recognized university in Asia.
KKU offers a wide range of programs: its comprehensive academic program offers 105 undergraduate majors,
along with 129 master's degree programs, and 59 doctoral programs.
Khon Kaen University was ranked 21st in Southeast Asia by Time Higher Education in 2009,
and 4th in Thailand by The Office of Higher Education Commission
